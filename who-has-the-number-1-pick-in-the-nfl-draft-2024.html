History of the NFL Draft Number 1 Pick

The NFL draft has a rich history dating back to its inception in 1936.

Over the years, the number 1 pick has been a coveted position, with teams aiming to secure a transformative talent that can elevate their franchise to new heights.

The Chicago Bears, a storied franchise in the NFL, have a unique history with the number 1 pick, having held it twice in the past.

In the 1941 draft, the Bears made their first number 1 pick, selecting Michigan halfback Tom Harmon.

Despite his decorated college career and winning the Heisman Trophy, Harmon never played for the Bears due to his involvement in World War II.

Six years later, in 1947, the Bears once again had the number 1 overall selection and drafted Oklahoma State halfback Bob Fenimore.

This historical context sets the stage for the significance of the number 1 pick in the NFL draft.

The Chicago Bears and the 2024 NFL Draft
