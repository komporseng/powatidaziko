Around midnight on September 12-13, fighting broke out along the shared border between Armenia and Azerbaijan when Azerbaijani forces started airstrikes and ground assaults into Armenia, primarily focusing on military installations and populated areas. ‘Provocations’ by Armenia, according to Azerbaijan, prompted the attacks.

After Armenia reported that a truce had been reached, it came to an end. The US government ‘played a big role’ in ending the fighting, according to Yerevan. The deal has not been formally accepted by the Azerbaijani side.

The two-day conflict was distinct from many earlier conflicts between the two nations in that Nagorno-Karabakh was not involved. In contrast to previous conflicts, the majority of the international community, mainly Western nations, appeared to single out Azerbaijan as the perpetrator and urged them to stop their attacks.

US Secretary of State Antony Blinken condemned the strikes within Armenia and urged President Ilham Aliyev of Azerbaijan to “cease the hostilities” and “disengage military forces” as fighting raged on Wednesday.

Blinken vowed to “deal with the issue” personally and assist in bringing the situation back to normal during a phone discussion with Nikol Pashinyan, the prime minister of Armenia.

Additionally, France has criticized Azerbaijan. The French envoy asked Azerbaijan to withdraw its forces from Armenian territory and “respect Armenia’s territorial integrity” at a meeting of the UN Security Council on Thursday.

Paruyr Hovhannisyan, Armenia’s deputy foreign minister, told Armenpress that the Security Council resolution’s adoption was now under consideration.

Russia, a longtime strategic partner of Armenia, responded less forcefully, calling for an end to hostilities on “both sides.”

Due to a bilateral agreement and their participation in the Collective Security Treaty Organization (CSTO), Russia is obligated to support Armenia militarily in the case of an assault on its territory. The CSTO deployed just a fact-finding mission, which arrived in Armenia on Friday, despite Armenia’s request for assistance.

Armen Grigoryan, the secretary of the Armenian Security Council, declared that the nation ‘does not have any more hope’ that the defense mechanisms of the treaty would be activated to assist Armenia.
