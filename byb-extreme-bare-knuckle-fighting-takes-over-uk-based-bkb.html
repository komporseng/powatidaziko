BYB Extreme Bare Knuckle Fighting Acquires UK-Based BKB

BYB Extreme Bare Knuckle Fighting Series has made a groundbreaking move by acquiring the world’s first professional bare knuckle boxing promotion, London-based BKB, thus becoming the largest company in the bare-knuckle space in terms of roster, library, and global reach. The acquisition, announced by BYB Owner and President Mike Vazquez, signifies a significant shift in the landscape of bare-knuckle fighting globally. The deal brings together two prominent organizations and is expected to have a substantial impact on the combat sports world

This acquisition has solidified BYB's position as the largest company in the bare knuckle space, boasting an extensive roster, library, and global reach.

Background of BYB and BKB

  • BYB Extreme Bare Knuckle Fighting Series: Based in Miami, Florida, BYB Extreme is a prominent US-based bare-knuckle organization that has been making waves in the combat sports world. The acquisition of BKB marks a major milestone for the company, solidifying its position as a leader in advancing the sport of bare knuckle fighting both domestically and internationally.
  • Bare Knuckle Boxing (BKB): Founded in 2015 by Jim Freeman and Joe Smith-Brown, BKB is known as the world’s first professional bare knuckle boxing promotion. Boasting a roster of over 175 international fighters, BKB has hosted numerous successful events and has been instrumental in professionalizing the sport in the modern era

A Historic Acquisition

In a recent announcement, Mike Vazquez, the Owner and President of BYB Extreme Bare Knuckle Fighting Series, revealed the acquisition of BKB, a renowned London-based bare knuckle boxing promotion.

This move marks a significant milestone in the history of bare knuckle fighting, as it brings together two prominent entities in the sport.

A Rich Legacy

Established in 2015 by Jim Freeman and Joe Smith-Brown, BKB holds the distinction of being the world's first professional bare knuckle boxing promotion.

Over the years, the organization has successfully organized forty bare knuckle events, captivating audiences.

With a strong following and millions of fans worldwide, BKB has carved a nice niche for itself in the realm of bare knuckle fighting.

Notable Achievements

BKB's influence extends beyond the UK, with international fights held in Phuket, Thailand, and a roster comprising over 175 international fighters.

Among its stellar lineup are some of the best bare knuckle fighters globally, including notable names such as Jimmy Sweeney from Ireland, Marko Martinjak from Croatia, and Barrie Jones and Dan Chapman from Wales. 

Strategic Leadership

Following the acquisition, both Jim Freeman and Joe Smith-Brown will assume executive roles at BYB. Freeman will oversee day-to-day operations for BYB in Europe, while Smith-Brown will continue to drive the organization's European expansion.

This strategic move aims to leverage the expertise and experience of the BKB leadership within the framework of BYB's global vision.

Unprecedented Global Impact

Mike Vazquez expressed his enthusiasm about the acquisition, emphasizing the unparalleled potential for expanding the reach of bare knuckle fighting on a global scale.

With a combined roster of over 250 fighters hailing from six continents and more than two dozen countries, BYB's influence in the bare knuckle arena has reached new heights. Also, the organization's extensive broadcasting on various platforms worldwide has brought the sport to millions of viewers globally.

Broadcasting Expansion

In a parallel development, BYB CEO Gregory Bloom announced the organization's expanded broadcasting presence, with live coverage on prominent networks such as Fuse, Fight Network, and BeIN SPORTS XTRA.

This strategic move underscores the growing popularity and commercial viability of bare knuckle fighting, positioning BYB as a dominant force in the combat sports landscape.

Pioneering the Future

With the acquisition of BKB, BYB has set its sights on propelling bare knuckle fighting into the mainstream, akin to the trajectory witnessed by the UFC and MMA two decades ago.

Gregory Bloom highlighted BYB's unwavering commitment to upholding the highest standards of professionalism and ownership, positioning the organization for widespread appeal and recognition.

A Testament to Growth

Joe Smith-Brown reflected on the journey of BKB and expressed pride in paving the way for the international expansion of bare knuckle fighting.

The acquisition by BYB signifies a testament to the company's remarkable growth and sets the stage for a promising future, offering both fighters and fans an unparalleled platform in the bare knuckle arena.

Embracing Evolution

BYB's evolution from backyard fights to a structured and internationally recognized product exemplifies the transformative power of the sport.

The organization's relentless efforts to expand the fanbase and global footprint of bare knuckle fighting continue to reshape the landscape of combat sports.

In the end, the acquisition of BKB by BYB Extreme Bare Knuckle Fighting Series marks a pivotal moment in the history of bare knuckle fighting, ushering in a new era of unprecedented growth, global reach, and mainstream recognition.
