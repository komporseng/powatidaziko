American actress Charlene “Michael” Hyatt was born in Britain. She appeared on stages all around the United States before her work in film and television, particularly in Ragtime on Broadway.

She portrayed Dr. Noelle Akopian on Crazy Ex-Girlfriend, Brianna Barksdale on The Wire, and Detective Sheila Muncie on Ray Donovan.

Hyatt has a long history of performing on stage, on screen, and on television. Hyatt played for five months in the Broadway production of Ragtime early in her career.

In the Eclipsed play by Danai Gurira, which was performed in Los Angeles in 2009, she portrayed the part of Rita.

The Wire, an HBO series, featured Hyatt as Brianna Barksdale in its first four seasons. She played Connie Reubens, the SWAT team leader, in The Kill Point on Spike TV. She has appeared on both Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip and The West Wing, both produced by Aaron Sorkin. Along with these shows, she has had brief cameos on ER, 24, Law & Order, Six Feet Under, The Big Bang Theory, Veronica Mars, Oz, Criminal Minds, Joan of Arcadia, Dexter, and Glee.
